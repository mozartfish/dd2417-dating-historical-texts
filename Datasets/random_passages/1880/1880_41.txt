We can do nothing with the door; let us go back to the treasure room.” We turned and went, and as we passed it I perceived by the unfinished wall across the passage the basket of food which poor Foulata had carried. I took it up, and brought it with me to the accursed treasure chamber that was to be our grave. Then we returned and reverently bore in Foulata’s corpse, laying it on the floor by the boxes of coin. Next we seated ourselves, leaning our backs against the three stone chests which contained the priceless treasure. “Let us divide the food,” said Sir Henry, “so as to make it last as long as possible.” Accordingly we did so.
